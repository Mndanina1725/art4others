About Us

Art for Others is a student-led, non-profit organization dedicated to uniting communities through the power of creative ingenuity. Based in Regina and Saskatoon Saskatchewan, we offer a range of visual arts programs designed to inspire connection and inclusivity. Our initiatives bring together individuals from all backgrounds, creating meaningful relationships while promoting mental health and overall well-being.

Get to know the Team

Angelena Wu

Co-Founder

Angelena grew up in Regina, Saskatchewan and is currently a second-year Health Studies student at the University of Saskatchewan. Passionate about mental health advocacy, Angelena started Art for Others to inspire through creative expression. When she’s not studying, you’ll probably find her painting, hitting the gym with friends or rock climbing (which mostly involves her trying not to fall off the wall).

Martin Ugwu

Co-Founder

Growing up in Saskatoon, Saskatchewan, Martin aims to launch and expand Art4Others in his hometown as an inaugural member. One of his goals and aspirations is to one day contribute to changing the world for the better (and to one day bench 315 pounds). As a third-year kinesiology student at the University of Saskatchewan, Martin spends most of his free time either joking around and making memories with friends or staying active either by playing sports or hitting the gym.

Azaria Tottenham

Event coordinator

Having spent most of her life in Saskatoon, Azaria is excited to connect with the community and make a difference through the power of art. As an artist, Azaria’s favorite mediums are watercolor and beadwork, both of which have played a significant role in her journey of self-identity. Her connection to art has fostered a sense of belonging and a positive community that she hopes to share with others. Additionally, Azaria’s work with beadwork has enabled her to raise awareness and funds for causes close to her heart.

Mission

At Art for Others, our mission is to harness the impact of art and creative expression to connect and include diverse communities of all ages across Saskatchewan, fostering unity and shared understanding.
